The Story

In 2015, electric vehicles in India were not a market — they were a bet. Ather Energy was just getting started. The major OEMs were not paying attention. And a small team of engineers in Indore decided to build a full-size electric two-wheeler from scratch, on bootstrapped money, testing prototypes during late nights in a makeshift workshop.

That was Vert Motors.

"We built something that actually moved. A full-sized electric moped with a working outer design, tested on real roads. Not a concept. Not a render. A real vehicle."

How Far We Got

2015

Founded Vert Motors

Team of 7–10 engineers. Started with a clear product vision — affordable, practical electric moped for Indian urban roads.

2016

First Working Prototype

Built the first full-size working prototype. Outer design completed. Tested during nights to avoid traffic and attention.

2016

Economic Times Catapult — Top 8

Applied to ET Catapult, one of India's most competitive startup competitions. Made it through two rounds to the final 8 out of hundreds of entries. Pitched to investors and industry leaders on a national stage.

2017

Second Prototype & Fundraising Push

Completed a second prototype. Engaged potential investors. Despite the ET Catapult recognition, could not close a funding round. Bootstrapped money ran out.

2017

Wound Down

Without funding, the team could not continue. Vert Motors shut down. The market Vert was building for — affordable Indian EVs — is now worth billions. Ather, Ola Electric, and others proved the thesis right. We were just early and underfunded.
